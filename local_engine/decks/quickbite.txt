QuickBite — Pitch Deck (anonymised reconstruction)
Premium entertainment for the mobile moment.

Slide 1: Cover & positioning
QuickBite — Premium entertainment for the mobile moment.
A premium, mobile-native entertainment network. Premium, not a feed.

Slide 2: The mobile viewing gap
Social platforms own short attention, but not quality.
Streaming owns quality, but demands long, living-room TV sessions.
Premium video was built for the living room. We built it for mobile — for the in-between moment that no one else serves.

Slide 3: A new programming format
Premium shows delivered as 7–10-minute chapters.
Unscripted short formats. Daily five-minute essentials across news, sport and culture.
Designed so you can finish in one session, with chapters that pull you back.

Slide 4: Designed for the in-between moment
Built for commutes, queues, lunch breaks, waiting rooms, and second-screen viewing.
One focused session at a time. A personalized feed that always has your next episode ready.
Mobile-only by design, to protect focus. The product feels alive daily.

Slide 5: RotateView
RotateView: our patent-pending, dual-framed format that switches instantly between portrait and landscape.
This is our technology moat — a viewing experience no incumbent offers.

Slide 6: Launch with scale
50–60 premium shows at launch, growing to 175 shows in year one.
8,500 episodes in the first year. 3+ hours of fresh premium content every day.

Slide 7: Premium content requires premium investment
Content investment: $496.5M pre-launch; $600M in year one; $978M by year five (base case); $2.6B by year five (upside).
This funds a premium slate at scale.

Slide 8: Two revenue streams
Subscription is the engine: a light-ad tier at $4.99/month and an ad-free tier at $7.99/month.
The ad-supported tier carries the early majority of subscribers.

Slide 9: Launch demand from advertisers
$150M in advertising pre-sold across eight categories before launch.
Premium mobile video is a brand-safe, scarce alternative to social — advertiser commitment proves confidence before user adoption.

Slide 10: Five-year subscriber scenarios
Downside 11M subscribers; base case 20M; upside 70M.
A content-led flywheel: more subscribers fund more content, which attracts more subscribers.

Slide 11: Backed by content, technology and brand
$1B+ secured. Elite creators on board. Carrier and platform distribution partnerships.
Led by a famous founder and a former technology CEO. This is a tech product, not a TV network.

Slide 12: Launch plan — build habit fast
Volume of content + personalization + brand-safe advertising will build a daily habit.
Success measured by net paid subscribers and engagement, not launch impressions.

Slide 13: Investment thesis — the five wedges
Five wedges into the market: format, content, technology, commercial, and scale.
The ask: funding to launch and capture the mobile entertainment category.

The moat — owned research

What closes the rest of the gap

The remaining gap on a small model is structural contradiction detection. Generic AI can't do this rigorously — but three lines of our own published research compose into exactly the missing capability:

AMR-LDA (ACL 2024)

Prose → logic

Normalises a claim into logical form and is sensitive to logical equivalence (contrapositive / De Morgan) — catching contradictions surface AI misses.

lemo / PARARULE-Plus

Rigorous verifier

A multi-hop deductive entailment classifier, robust to rule perturbations — confirms real contradictions and rejects false ones.

RLearner / Explanation-Gen

Verified + faithful

A generator–verifier RL loop makes findings verified-by-construction and their explanations faithful.

Now live in the app, not a slide: every LLM-proposed contradiction is re-checked by the logic verifier (NLI) and tagged ✓ verified / ♻ duplicate / ✗ unverified — so near-duplicates the model mislabels as "contradictions" no longer read as real ones. It runs on the server and degrades gracefully on a laptop (skips if no GPU stack; raw bonds still shown).

The AMR-LDA equivalence layer is wired in behind it (offline stage): on real decks it correctly collapses logically-equivalent paraphrases with zero false positives after a bidirectional-equivalence check — validated, not asserted. Its proven win today is rigorous de-duplication; reliable new-contradiction discovery on a small model is what the distillation below is built to deliver.

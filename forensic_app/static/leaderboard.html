Forensic Engine — Model Evaluation

An internal, measured study of which local open-weights model best drives the engine — scored on recall (catching an expert's flaws) and precision (staying quiet on clean documents). ~36 models across 4 sweeps. Last updated 2026-07-11.

Verdict: gemma4:26b (thinking off) is the production pick.
79% recall / 1 false-positive on the broadened set (after the trigger-pack precision fix) — beating the previous engine model qwen2.5-coder:32b (74% / 12) on both axes. Efficient alternative: gpt-oss:20b (74% / 2, MoE 3.6B active). Zero-false-positive, no-container option: general qwen2.5:32b (71% / 0).

Model strengths & weaknesses

gemma4:26b — the pick

  • + best recall/precision balance (79% / 1 after fix)
  • + thinking-off → simple, no reasoning latency; ~16GB
  • + router fires correctly; balanced across every case
  • − needs the newer Ollama (runs in the Docker 0.30.10 container)

gpt-oss:20b — the efficient one

  • + 74% / 2; MoE, only 3.6B active → fast on modest hardware
  • + reasoning-native: thinking helps its precision
  • − needs thinking on (slower per call) + newer Ollama

qwen2.5:32b (general) — the safe one

  • + 0 false-positives; 71% recall
  • + runs on the existing system Ollama — no container dependency
  • − ~8 recall points behind gemma4:26b

The high-recall / high-FP tier

  • deepseek-r1:14b (80%/15), yi:34b (77%/20), qwen3:14b (79%/16), gemma3 (79%/18–31)
  • − they reach high recall by flagging aggressively — the FP cost is not worth ~3 recall points

mistral-small — the cautionary tale

  • Looked like the winner on a single clean doc (80%/0), then fell to 71%/9 on the broadened set — it over-applies the medical rules even with the precision guard.
  • Why broadening the eval mattered: one clean doc was luck.

Small models (≤10B) & qwen3-hybrid

  • Small models (phi4-mini, glm4:9b, tulu3, hermes3) over-fire badly on clean docs (FP 8–50).
  • qwen3 hybrid thinking, turned on, lowers recall (argues itself charitable).

What the study actually proved

  • "Reason harder" is not the lever. A generic deeper-reasoning pass moved recall ~15%→18%; turning a hybrid model's own thinking up lowered recall (76%→69%).
  • Importing an external frame IS the lever. Routed domain rulebooks took recall from ~15% to ~75%; a held-out clinical pitch went 0/9 → 9/9. Depth didn't do that — a foreign frame did.
  • Thinking, done right, helps precision. Purpose-built reasoners (gpt-oss, qwq) give the best precision; hybrid-toggle thinking (qwen3) hurts recall. It's which reasoning, not whether.
  • The biggest lever is the rulebook, not the model. Fixing where the packs over-fire on clean clinical text cut false-positives more than any model swap (gemma4:26b FP 7→1).
  • False-positives, not recall, separate the models — recall bunches in the noise band; precision does not.
  • ~36 models tested; none beats gemma4:26b. The model is a secondary lever behind the encoded rules, the precision fix, and routing.

Reasoning research — routing, not depth (the "caterpillar" work)

The frontier isn't a deeper analyser (buyable with compute) — it's knowing where to dig. Three mechanisms built and measured:

  • Collision detector — forces claims that sit pages apart into contact. On Christoph's Kaiora deck, from the neutral decomposition and with zero hints, our engine independently recovered 5 of the 6 load-bearing findings from the day-long manual takedown — including the "LTV/CAC ratio asserted with no working" that manually needed a human to spot. (The one miss, a deleted chart axis, is a visual-only fact — an external-frame caterpillar.)
  • Confidence-grounding meter — the mechanical "whoa": flags claims whose confidence outruns their support. That habitat covers 78% of all gold flaws; the meter catches them and stays quiet on genuinely-hedged writing.
  • Habitat-annotated corpus (63 flaws) — turns "find every flaw" into an ethology of where flaws live, so routing is measurable.

Two honest results that shape the design: (1) flaws are LEAF nodes, not central hubs — the naive "dig the high-centrality node" heuristic is rejected by the data; the useful signal is the collision/confidence-gap marker on a specific claim. (2) A learned bait-routing policy matches "run everything" on recall but only pays over a habitat-diverse corpus — on a homogeneous set a cheap fixed heuristic wins, so the corpus is the next bottleneck. Across the board these are routing signals (where to look) that must verify against the source before charging — used as blanket finding-generators they add no recall and cost precision.
